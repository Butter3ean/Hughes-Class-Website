Capybaras


Did you know the scientific name of a capybara is Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris?

According to Capybara Population by Country 2024

Description

There are 1.2 million capybaras in Brazil. Their face looks like a beaver's. Adults can grow to 2 feet in height and 4.4 feet in length. They are generally shy but will associate in groups along the banks of lakes and rivers. They usually live in forests near bodies of water such as rivers, lakes, swamps, flooded savannas, or in trpical rainforests. fun fact: some animals use capybaras as furniture.



Diet

Capybaras eat 6-8 pounds of grass a day. Predators of capybaras are caimans, anacondas, jaguars, ocelots, harpy eagles, and humans. Some adaptations of capybaras are their eyes and nose atop their head and their webbed feet to help them spend more time in the water and to help them swim.

Scientific Name:

Sciuridae

Appearance

Color: Red, Brown, Grey, or black

Unique Characteristics: A squirrels front teeth never stop growing

Physical Features: Long bodies, soft fine hair, (although some have very thick hair) ,and large eyes

Height and Weight: Their height sitting up straight would typically fall somewhere around 15 inches and they weigh around one pound-- same size as a chipmunk

How They Move: A weaving run or a series of leaps with the tail held out behind

How They Eat: Squirrels use their incisors something akin to a crowbar; they gnaw a small hole in the top of the nut into which they insert their incisors and 'jimmy' it open from the top, causing it to split roughly in half with clean edges

How They Communicate: They communicate with squawk- like calls. They are quite vocal creatures. In some cases, squirrels even talk to predators warning them to stay away

How They Intereact to Their Enviroment: Important predators of seeds and other animals in the ecosystems in which they live

Interesting Facts About Their Behavior: Squirrels are extremely vocal- they bark, chatter, scream, and purr to communicate with one another. They also communicate through body language, by moving their tails and stomping their feet

Natural Habitat

Where They typically Live: Squirrels usually nest outside in tree trunks or under porches. Mostly in forest

How Does Their Enviroment Suit Them: Wooded areas with an abundance of high-quality canopy and food

Cool Facts About Their Habitat And How It Adapts To Its Enviroment: Squirrels make nests, called dreys, using twigs, leaves, moss, and other natural objects. Look for them at the top of a large tree. These critters are active throughout the year and are mostly seen during the day

Information

What They Eat: They eat seeds and nuts, supplemented with fruits, corn, insects, and bark.

They are omnivores

Squirrels adapt their diets based on availability, and will meat, and garbage if nessary

What Are Their Main Threats They Are Facing: Humans represent the greatest threat to gray squirrels.

Interesing Facts That Help Them Survive:Squirrels are mammals that have fur that can camouflage, or help them blend in with their surroundings and hide them from predators. They're extremely quick and flexible, and this is because their back feet rotate 180 degrees, which means they can turn from completely forward to completely backward.

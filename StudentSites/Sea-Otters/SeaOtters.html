Sea Otters

Scientific Name

Enhydra lutris

Appearance

Sea otters are part of the weasel family. They have webbed feet,water-repellent fur to keep them dry and warm, and nostrills and ears that close in the water.

Size and Weight

Adult sea otters may grow up to 5 feet in length. The males weigh 80-100 pounds but can weigh more than 100 pounds. The females are smaller, weighing 50-70 pounds.

Behavior

Sea otters are social species-females and their pup are often observed spemdimg time together in one group and males in the other

Habitat

The Sea Otter lives in area where it can drive for food in the Pacific Ocean, most often within one to two kilometers from shore where the water is no more than 40 meters deep

Sounds

Otters are known for their unique sounds such as chirping, purring, squealing and making gurgling sounds

Diet

Their main prey species include sea urchins, crabs, clams, mussels, octopus, fish, and other marine invertebrates. Sea otter teeth are adapted for crushing hard-shelled invertebrates such as clams, urchins, and crabs. They are carnivores, and, at times, omnivorous, meaning they feed on both animal and plant food.

Predator and Threats

Major non-human predators of sea otters include sharks, killer whales, coyotes, brown bears and even eagles. Southern sea otters have been killed illegally because of the belief that they compete with commercial fishers and divers collecting sea urchins commercially.
